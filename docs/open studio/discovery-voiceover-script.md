# Open Studio Discovery — Voiceover Script
*Kit's inner monologue narration + spoken dialogue*

---

## How the Two Layers Work

There are two types of audio in the Discovery experience:

### 1. NARRATION (Inner Monologue)
Kit's private thoughts. Plays automatically as panels scroll into view. The student hears Kit thinking — vulnerabilities, observations, memories that Kit wouldn't say out loud. This is the Max Payne layer. Warm, reflective, slightly self-aware.

**Voice direction for ElevenLabs:** Warm, mid-range, slightly rough. Think a thoughtful craftsperson talking to themselves — not performing, not broadcasting. Intimate mic feel, like they're right next to you. Occasional half-laugh. Natural pauses. NOT a podcast host, NOT a teacher, NOT a narrator. A real person thinking out loud.

### 2. DIALOGUE (Speech Bubbles)
Kit talking directly to the student. These match the speech bubble text on screen. Can be pre-generated for structural panels or generated in real-time for AI response panels.

**Voice direction:** Same voice, but warmer and more direct. Slightly more energy than the narration. Kit is now talking TO someone, not to themselves. Still casual — like a colleague, not a teacher giving instructions.

## CHAPTER 1: "The Workshop" (Strengths)

### Panel 1.1 — THE DOOR
*[Silent image. No speech bubbles. The door, warm light, "Studio — come in" sign.]*

**NARRATION (auto-plays as panel appears, 2-second delay after scroll):**

> "Another new one. [beat] They always pause at the door. I used to think it was nerves. Now I think it's something else. They're deciding whether this is going to be real — or just another adult asking them to perform."

> "Let's find out."

**[Duration: ~12 seconds. Tone: quiet, observational, a hint of self-awareness.]**

---

### Panel 1.2 — KIT'S INTRO
*[Kit leaning against workbench. Speech bubble on screen.]*

**DIALOGUE (matches speech bubble):**

> "So — welcome. I know this is a bit unusual. You've been asked to 'discover yourself' — which sounds like something off a wellness retreat brochure."

> [slight laugh]

> "I promise this is different."

**[Duration: ~10 seconds]**

**NARRATION (plays after dialogue, during thought bubble appearance):**

> "Just be honest with them. They'll know if you're not."

**[Duration: ~3 seconds. Quieter, more internal.]**

---

### Panel 1.3a — YOUNG KIT (Failure)
*[Kit in their 20s, surrounded by failed prototypes.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "When I was starting out, I spent a year trying to be a graphic designer. Because it seemed like the right career. I was terrible. [beat] Absolutely terrible."

**[Duration: ~8 seconds. Tone: self-deprecating, honest, slight wince in the voice.]**

---

### Panel 1.3b — KIT NOW (Reflection)
*[Kit pointing at finished project, looking at the mess fondly.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "But I kept noticing something. Every night, I'd sneak away from the screen to build things with my hands instead. Little models. Prototypes. Things nobody asked for."

> "Took me an embarrassingly long time to realise — the thing I kept avoiding my real work to do... was my real work."

**[Duration: ~14 seconds. Tone: warm, the realisation still landing even now.]**

**NARRATION (after dialogue):**

> "I tell that story a lot. It never stops being true. The thing you can't stop doing — that's the signal. Most people spend years ignoring it."

**[Duration: ~8 seconds]**

---

### Panel 1.4 — THE PROVOCATION
*[Messy workbench, scenario card, Kit stepping back with coffee.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "Here's what I want to know. And there's genuinely no right answer."

> "Your best friend comes to you, panicking. Big project. Due tomorrow. Two hours left. They have no idea where to start."

> [beat]

> "What do you actually do? Not what you think you should do. What do you actually do?"

**[Duration: ~16 seconds. Tone: genuinely curious. The pause before "What do you actually do?" matters — it signals this isn't a test.]**

**NARRATION (plays while student is typing):**

> "This is the question that tells me everything. Not because of what they say — but because of what they don't even think to mention. The maker grabs materials before they've finished reading the brief. The researcher opens six tabs. The leader starts drawing a plan. They don't even know they're telling me who they are."

**[Duration: ~14 seconds. Tone: observational, fascinated. This plays quietly under the input area while the student thinks.]**

---

### Panel 1.5 — KIT REFLECTS ON THE ANSWER
*[Close-up of Kit, leaning forward, genuinely interested.]*

**DIALOGUE (AI-generated, responds to student's answer):**

> [This is generated in real-time by the API and converted to speech via ElevenLabs. Not pre-recorded.]

**NARRATION (plays after AI dialogue):**

> "There it is. They don't see it yet — but I do. [beat] That instinct they just described? That's not a random preference. That's wiring."

**[Duration: ~7 seconds]**

---

### Panel 1.6 — GOING DEEPER
*[Kit on a stool, more intimate framing.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "One more thing. The thing people actually come to you for. Not what you wish they'd ask — what they really come to you for. Could be anything. Fixing tech. Settling arguments. Making things look good. Being honest when everyone else is being polite."

**[Duration: ~14 seconds. Tone: direct but warm. "Being honest when everyone else is being polite" should land with a slight knowing smile in the voice.]**

---

### Panel 1.7 — CHAPTER CLOSE
*[Wide workshop shot, late afternoon light. Sketchbook with notes.]*

**NARRATION:**

> "Two answers. That's all it took. I already know more about them than most icebreakers learn in an hour. [beat] Not because I asked the right questions — because I asked the real ones."

**[Duration: ~9 seconds. Tone: satisfied, quiet.]**

**DIALOGUE (as profile reveal card slides in):**

> "I'm starting to get a picture of you. Let's keep going."

**[Duration: ~4 seconds]**

## CHAPTER 2: "The Collection Wall" (Interests)

### Panel 2.1 — TRANSITION
*[Kit walking through workshop toward back wall. No dialogue.]*

**NARRATION:**

> "Strengths are what you can do. But they're not the whole picture. I need to know what pulls them forward — the things they'd do even if nobody was watching. Even if it didn't count for anything."

**[Duration: ~10 seconds. Tone: reflective, walking pace.]**

---

### Panel 2.2 — THE WALL
*[The collection wall — dense with pinned items.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "This is my collection wall. Everything on here is something that made me stop and go... huh. That's interesting. No theme. No logic. Just things that grabbed me."

> "That circuit board? Found it on a street in Shenzhen. The kid's drawing? My niece's version of this workshop. The bridge photo? I don't even know why I like that bridge. I just do."

**[Duration: ~18 seconds. Tone: casual, a little nerdy, genuinely affectionate about the objects.]**

---

### Panel 2.3 — KIT'S CONFESSION
*[Close-up, slightly sheepish.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "People say 'follow your passion' like it's one big obvious thing. [beat] I've never had that. I have a hundred small curiosities that bump into each other. The interesting stuff happens in the collisions."

**[Duration: ~10 seconds. Tone: honest admission. The beat after "one big obvious thing" carries weight — Kit is debunking a myth that paralyses teenagers.]**

**NARRATION:**

> "I say this because almost every student I've worked with thinks there's something wrong with them for not having One Big Passion. There isn't. Most interesting people are a mess of overlapping curiosities. The trick is noticing the pattern."

**[Duration: ~11 seconds]**

---

### Panel 2.4 — THE INVITATION
*[Blank card pinned to wall, marker offered.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "If you had a wall like this — what would be on it? And I don't mean school subjects. I don't mean 'I like science.' I mean... what do you actually spend time on when nobody's watching? What rabbit hole did you fall into last? What makes you completely lose track of time?"

**[Duration: ~15 seconds. Tone: inviting, leaning in. "When nobody's watching" is the key phrase — say it like it matters.]**

---

### Panel 2.5 — KIT CONNECTS THE DOTS
*[Kit's hand moving pins, drawing connections. Then surprised/delighted expression.]*

**DIALOGUE (AI-generated, real-time):**

> [Responds to student's answer. Connects interest to earlier strength.]

**NARRATION (after AI dialogue):**

> "And there's the collision. They don't see it yet — how the thing they're good at and the thing they care about are the same thread. But it's right there. It's always right there."

**[Duration: ~9 seconds]**

---

### Panel 2.6 — WHAT ANNOYS YOU?
*[Kit sitting on floor, sketching casually.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "Okay. Different angle. What annoys you? Like genuinely bugs you about the world? Not 'world peace' level stuff — I mean something specific. Something that's broken, or dumb, or unfair. Something you notice that other people don't seem to care about."

**[Duration: ~14 seconds. Tone: casual but pointed. Kit is on the floor — physically lower, more equal. The voice should match that — less "mentor", more "friend asking a real question".]**

**NARRATION (while student types):**

> "Passion is abstract. Irritation is specific. Ask a teenager what they're passionate about and they freeze. Ask them what annoys them and you can't shut them up. [beat] That's where the real projects hide."

**[Duration: ~10 seconds]**

---

### Panel 2.7 — CHAPTER CLOSE
*[Collection wall with student's items added alongside Kit's.]*

**DIALOGUE (as profile reveal slides in):**

> "I like your wall. It's different from mine — but I can see the thread."

**[Duration: ~5 seconds. Tone: genuine appreciation, not praise.]**

**NARRATION:**

> "Two chapters in and I can already feel the shape of something forming. They can't see it yet. That's fine. That's my job for now."

**[Duration: ~7 seconds]**

---

## CHAPTER 3: "The Window" (Needs)

### Panel 3.1 — THE WINDOW
*[Two silhouettes at window, community scene below.]*

**NARRATION (auto-plays, no dialogue on this panel):**

> "Everything so far has been about them. What they're good at. What they care about. Now comes the turn. [beat] The hardest part of any project isn't the making. It's noticing something worth making in the first place."

**[Duration: ~12 seconds. Tone: thoughtful, a shift in energy — more grounded, slightly more serious.]**

---

### Panel 3.2 — THE MEDICINE BOTTLE STORY
*[Elderly hands struggling with childproof cap.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "A few years ago, a student of mine — Year 9, quiet kid, good with her hands — spent a week walking around her neighbourhood with a camera. Not for an assignment. Just looking."

> "She came back with this one photo. Her grandmother. Trying to open a medicine bottle. One of those childproof caps."

> [beat]

> "Her nana had arthritis. She couldn't open her own medicine. Someone designed that bottle — and never once thought about her hands."

**[Duration: ~22 seconds. Tone: storytelling pace. The beat before "Her nana had arthritis" is important — let the image do the work for a moment before Kit explains it. The final line should land quietly, almost to himself.]**

**NARRATION:**

> "That one photo became a six-month project. She redesigned the cap. It was brilliant. And it all started with just... looking."

**[Duration: ~7 seconds]**

---

### Panel 3.3 — THE SHIFT
*[Kit turns from window to face student.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "So far we've been talking about you — what you're good at, what interests you. Now I want to flip it."

> "Who around you needs something? Not a charity case — someone whose day you could genuinely make better. Could be a person, a group, your school, your street, your family."

> "Who comes to mind when you think... that shouldn't be that hard?"

**[Duration: ~16 seconds. Tone: the energy shifts from reflective to direct. "That shouldn't be that hard" is the hook — say it like it's a thought that's been bugging Kit personally.]**

**NARRATION (while student types):**

> "This is where the magic happens — if it's going to happen. When a teenager connects what they're good at with someone who actually needs it... that's not a school project anymore. That's purpose."

**[Duration: ~9 seconds]**

---

### Panel 3.4 — KIT BRIDGES
*[Kit drawing on whiteboard, connecting strengths + interests + needs.]*

**DIALOGUE (AI-generated, real-time):**

> [Connects all three elements. Thinks out loud.]

**NARRATION:**

> "Three things. A strength, a fascination, and a need. When those three collide, you don't get a project — you get a reason."

**[Duration: ~7 seconds]**

---

### Panel 3.5 — CHAPTER CLOSE (Silent)
*[The window again, warmer light. One person in the scene subtly highlighted.]*

**NARRATION:**

> "I'm not going to tell them what to do with this. Not yet. Let it sit. Let the collision do its work."

**[Duration: ~6 seconds. Tone: patient, trusting the process.]**

---

## CHAPTER 4: "The Crossroads" (Narrowing)

### Panel 4.1 — THREE DOORS
*[Corridor with three distinct doors.]*

**NARRATION (before dialogue):**

> "Here's where they get scared. Every single time. Three possibilities — and choosing one means killing two. Teenagers hate this. [beat] Adults hate this too. We're just better at pretending we don't."

**[Duration: ~10 seconds. Tone: knowing, empathetic, slightly amused.]**

**DIALOGUE:**

> "Based on everything you've told me, I see three possible directions. None of them are wrong. But you can only walk through one door."

> "Let's look at each one."

**[Duration: ~8 seconds]**

---

### Panels 4.2–4.4 — DOOR EXPLORATIONS
*[Kit opens each door. AI generates descriptions based on student profile.]*

**DIALOGUE (AI-generated per door):**

> [For each door: brief description of the project direction, one feasibility question, one excitement question.]

**NARRATION (between doors, quiet):**

After Door 1:
> "Watch their face. [beat] ...well, watch their words. You can tell a lot by how fast they type."

**[Duration: ~5 seconds]**

After Door 2:
> "They're comparing. That's good. The worst thing would be if they said yes to the first one without looking."

**[Duration: ~5 seconds]**

---

### Panel 4.5 — KIT ON CHOOSING
*[Kit leaning against door frame, honest expression.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "Can I tell you something? I have never once in my life picked the right project on the first try. Every project I'm proud of started as something completely different."

> "The magic isn't in choosing perfectly. It's in committing hard enough that the project teaches you what it actually wants to be."

> [beat]

> "So don't agonise. Pick the one that makes your stomach do a little flip. That's your signal."

**[Duration: ~18 seconds. Tone: this is Kit's most important speech. Honest, grounded, hard-won wisdom delivered casually. "Makes your stomach do a little flip" should have a slight warmth — Kit remembers that feeling.]**

**NARRATION:**

> "Choosing is a skill. Not a talent — a skill. They'll get better at it every time. But this first one has to be their own."

**[Duration: ~6 seconds]**

---

### Panel 4.6 — THE CHOICE
*[Chosen door, open, light pouring through.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "That one?"

**[Duration: ~1 second. Simple. Let it land.]**

**NARRATION (after student confirms):**

> "Good. [beat] Now we go somewhere."

**[Duration: ~3 seconds. Tone: quiet confidence. Not celebration — forward motion.]**

---

## CHAPTER 5: "The Launchpad" (Commitment)

### Panel 5.1 — THE ASCENT
*[Climbing stairs, words chalked on steps. No dialogue.]*

**NARRATION:**

> "Strengths. Interests. A need in the world. A direction chosen. Four steps. [beat] That's more clarity than most adults have about their careers."

> "One step left. The hardest one. Say it out loud. Make it real."

**[Duration: ~11 seconds. Tone: building energy, almost proud but holding it back.]**

---

### Panel 5.2 — THE VIEW
*[Panoramic rooftop/hilltop. Two silhouettes. Morning light.]*

**NARRATION (auto-plays, slow):**

> "I love this moment. Right before they say it. They know what they're going to do — they just haven't put words around it yet. The words make it real. The words make it scary. [beat] The words make it theirs."

**[Duration: ~12 seconds. Tone: quiet reverence. This is the emotional peak.]**

**DIALOGUE:**

> "Alright. We're here."

> "You know what you're good at. You know what fires you up. You know who needs what you've got."

> [beat]

> "Now say it. Not for me — for you. What are you going to make happen?"

**[Duration: ~12 seconds. Tone: elevated but not dramatic. "What are you going to make happen?" is an invitation, not a command.]**

---

### Panel 5.3 — THE COMMITMENT
*[Sketchbook, pen, warm light.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "Write it like this: I'm going to — what. For — who. Because — why it matters to me. One sentence. Don't overthink it."

**[Duration: ~8 seconds. Tone: practical, grounding. After the elevated moment, Kit brings it back to earth with a simple instruction.]**

---

### Panel 5.4 — KIT'S RESPONSE
*[Kit reading the sketchbook. Genuine expression.]*

**DIALOGUE (AI-generated, real-time):**

> [Reflects the project statement back. Connects it to the full journey.]

**NARRATION (after AI dialogue):**

> "There's this moment — right after they say it — where something shifts in them. They sit up a little straighter. Their voice gets a little steadier. [beat] They just went from 'I don't know what to do' to 'I know exactly what I'm doing.' In five chapters."

> "That never gets old."

**[Duration: ~14 seconds. Tone: warm pride, not for Kit's work but for the student's arrival.]**

---

### Panel 5.5 — THE SEND-OFF
*[Kit at rooftop edge, casual wave, backlit by morning light.]*

**DIALOGUE:**

> "I'll be around. When you're stuck, when you're flying, when you want to quit — come find me. I'll be in the workshop."

> [beat]

> "Now go build something."

**[Duration: ~9 seconds. Tone: casual confidence. "Now go build something" is the button. Land it clean. No sentimentality.]**

**NARRATION (final, as profile card rises):**

> "And off they go. [beat] I'll see them again soon enough. Probably stuck. Probably frustrated. Probably wondering why they picked this project."

> "That's when the real work starts."

**[Duration: ~9 seconds. Tone: knowing smile. The mentor's work isn't done — it's barely begun. This sets up the next phase without being a cliffhanger.]**

## Production Notes

### Pre-Recorded vs Real-Time

**Pre-record these (same for every student):**
- All NARRATION lines (Kit's inner monologue) — ~25 clips
- All DIALOGUE on structural panels (1.1, 1.2, 1.3a, 1.3b, 1.4, 1.6, 2.1–2.4, 2.6, 3.1–3.3, 4.1, 4.5, 5.1–5.3, 5.5) — ~20 clips
- Total pre-recorded: ~45 clips, roughly 5–6 minutes of audio

**Generate in real-time via ElevenLabs API:**
- All AI-response dialogue (1.5, 2.5, 3.4, 4.2–4.4, 5.4) — 6–8 clips per session
- These are the panels where Kit responds to what the student actually said
- API call: student submits → backend generates text response → sends text to ElevenLabs TTS → returns audio URL alongside text

### ElevenLabs Settings

**Voice selection:** Pick or clone a voice that matches the direction — warm, mid-range, slightly textured. Not too smooth (that reads as corporate), not too rough (that reads as tired). The "Adam" or "Antoni" stock voices are decent starting points, but a custom clone from a real person would be ideal.

**Settings for NARRATION clips:**
- Stability: 0.45 (slightly more expressive/variable)
- Similarity boost: 0.75
- Style: 0.3 (subtle emotional range)
- Speaker boost: on

**Settings for DIALOGUE clips:**
- Stability: 0.55 (a touch more consistent since these are direct speech)
- Similarity boost: 0.75
- Style: 0.2
- Speaker boost: on

### Audio Playback UX

- Panels auto-play audio when they scroll into the viewport (IntersectionObserver)
- Small speaker icon in corner of each panel — tap to replay, tap to mute
- Global mute toggle in the progress bar area
- If student is typing (input focused), narration audio plays at 40% volume — background ambiance, not competing
- On mobile: respect silent mode, show captions as fallback
- Audio clips should have 0.5s fade-in to avoid jarring starts

### Total Audio Budget
- Pre-recorded: ~45 clips, ~5-6 minutes total
- Real-time generated: 6-8 clips per student session, ~1-2 minutes
- Full experience audio: ~7-8 minutes across a 15-20 minute Discovery session
- Roughly 40% of the experience has audio; 60% is the student reading, thinking, and typing
