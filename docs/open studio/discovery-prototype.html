Chapter Two
The Collection Wall
What pulls you forward when nobody's watching?
▶ Kit — Inner Monologue (transition)
"Strengths are what you can do. But they're not the whole picture. I need to know what pulls them forward — the things they'd do even if nobody was watching. Even if it didn't count for anything."
📸 Panel 2.2: The Collection Wall — dense pinboard with photos, circuit board, kid's drawing, fabric swatches, postcards, bus tickets, handwritten notes, connected with string
🎨
"This is my collection wall. Everything on here is something that made me stop and go... huh. That's interesting. No theme. No logic. Just things that grabbed me."

"That circuit board? Found it on a street in Shenzhen. The kid's drawing? My niece's version of this workshop. The bridge photo? I don't even know why I like that bridge. I just do."
🎨
"People say 'follow your passion' like it's one big obvious thing. I've never had that. I have a hundred small curiosities that bump into each other. The interesting stuff happens in the collisions."
▶ Kit — Inner Monologue
"I say this because almost every student I've worked with thinks there's something wrong with them for not having One Big Passion. There isn't. Most interesting people are a mess of overlapping curiosities. The trick is noticing the pattern."
🎨
"If you had a wall like this — what would be on it? Not school subjects. I don't mean 'I like science.' I mean... what do you actually spend time on when nobody's watching? What rabbit hole did you fall into last? What makes you completely lose track of time?"
