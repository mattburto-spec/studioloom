The working drawing is a precise, 1:1 scale drawing that describes your car, its dimensions, and its features. 
Working drawings should have top and side, or profile, views. An accurate working drawing is important for two reasons: 1) A copy of the working drawing serves as a template for making a CAD model and 
2) rough-cutting your car blank.

Step 2: 
Draw the top and side on the grid paper (supplied) 
Make light projection lines from one view to the other to help you locate axle holes and other features of your design. Show the location of hidden details (such as the cartridge hole) by using dashed lines. Check the specifications.   Look at your design sketch to see how each spec applies to your design. You may find it necessary to take notes or even change your design.
