1. Mass (Weight) and Newton’s Laws
The Science:
 Look at 
Newton’s Second Law
: Force = Mass \times Acceleration (F=ma).
This can be rewritten as: 
Acceleration = Force / Mass
.
The Constant:
 Your CO2 cartridge provides a fixed amount of 
Force
.
Research Task:
 Find an image of a heavy "Steel Rim" and a light "Carbon Fiber Rim." Paste them below.
The Logic Test (Circle the correct answers):
 
If the 
Mass
 of the wheel increases, the 
Acceleration
 will ( Increase / Decrease ).
Which of Newton's Laws explains why a heavy car is harder to push than a light car? 
A) Newton's 1st Law 
B) Newton's 2nd Law 
C) Newton's 3rd Law 
Design Decision:

2. Width and Friction
The Science:
 
Friction
 is a force that opposes motion.
Research Task:
 Find an image of a "Wide Dragster Tire" and a "Narrow Solar Car Tire."
The Logic Test:
 
Wide tires have a large "Contact Patch" (area touching the ground).
Narrow tires have a small "Contact Patch."
Hypothesis:
 More contact with the ground creates ( More / Less ) friction.
Critical Thinking:
 Real dragsters use wide tires because they need grip to stop the wheels from spinning out. Your CO2 car is pushed by air, not by the wheels. Therefore: 
Do you need grip? ( Yes / No )
Should you choose wide or narrow wheels? ( Wide / Narrow )

---SLIDE---
3. Aerodynamics (Turbulence)
The Science:
 
Drag
 is the air resisting your car. Objects that are "smooth" allow air to pass easily (Laminar Flow). Objects with holes or complex shapes churn the air (Turbulence).
Research Task:
 Find an image of a "Spoked Bicycle Wheel" and a "Solid Disc Wheel."
The Logic Test:
 
Look at the spokes. As they spin, they act like a fan, hitting the air. This creates ( Drag / Thrust ).
Look at the solid disc. The air flows over the flat surface.
Design Decision:
 
To reduce air resistance, the most aerodynamic wheel shape is the ( Spoked / Solid ) wheel.
