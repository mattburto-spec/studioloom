# CO2 Racers — Agency Unit Foundation

> **Single reference document for the Year 9 CO2 Racers unit running 5 May → 11 June 2026.**
> Combines: design (v5), pedagogical foundations, last year's content analysis, and 3-lens research synthesis (Claude + Gemini + Cowork).
> Drafted 5 May 2026 across a single working session. Authoritative.

---

## TL;DR

**The unit isn't about cars. It's about agency.**

Year 9 students at NIS run a 14-class unit (60 min each) building CO2-powered balsa-wood race cars. They've done this build sequence before (Year 8) so they know the pipeline: sketch → working drawing → CAD wheels → 3D print (via Preflight) → bandsaw shape → sand → paint → assemble → race.

This iteration strips the scaffolding theatre from last year. The build pipeline stays. The pedagogical focus pivots to:

1. **Agency** — measured via the New Metrics framework, scaffolded through the Three Cs rubric (Choice / Causation / Change)
2. **Project Management** — Kanban with WIP=1, blockage triage, time-estimation loop, backward-mapped milestones
3. **Reflection** — 4-prompt journal × 14 classes, photo-of-decision, self-rereading prompts, calibration conversations

**3 hard anchors + 11 self-paced studio classes.** Students choose order + timing within structural constraints. Race day is celebration, not assessment. The metacurriculum (agency + PM + reflection) is graded ≥70%; the car ≤30%.

**Class of 9 students, individual (not group) work, individual journals + Kanbans + surveys.** All 3D printing routes through **Preflight** — first real classroom use of the fabrication pipeline.

**Build cost: ~4-5 days of StudioLoom work** before unit goes live: Kanban (lesson-linked, with PM features), backward-map Timeline, 4-prompt Journal block (using existing portfolio infra), Three Cs Survey block (5 surveys, milestone-triggered), Teacher Attention-Rotation Panel.

**Race day:** 11 June 2026.

## 2. The pedagogical pivot — what's different from last year

Last year's unit (read the full PPT extracts in `docs/units/extracted/`) had 5 sequential tasks:

1. **Task 1: Research** (4 lessons) — Newton's laws via fill-in-the-blank sentence starters, vocabulary, F=ma
2. **Task 2: Ideation** — 5 concept sketches, SCAMPER, biomimicry
3. **Technical Drawing** — 1:1 working drawing on grid paper
4. **Wheel Design** — Onshape CAD models
5. **Making** — bandsaw, sand, paint, assemble (with a "Step / Tools / Failures / Time" template)
6. **Final Evaluation** — 6 generic reflection prompts at the end

**What was wrong** (Matt's call, 5 May 2026):

- Heavy fill-in-the-blank scaffolding everywhere — students learned to fill blanks, not to make decisions
- Marking rubrics graded the FINAL ARTIFACT (drawing quality, sketch range, vocabulary count) — nothing about *why* students chose what they chose
- Reflection at the end was generic ("what did you learn? what advice for next year?") — disconnected from process
- Process journal was just template steps — descriptive chronicle, not reflective
- Implicit teamwork — no individual evidence trail
- Strong on physics, weak on agency

**What students retain from last year (assume ~70%):**

- Build pipeline + tool safety (bandsaw, drill press, sander)
- Newton's 3 laws (probably patchy on F=ma applications)
- SCAMPER as ideation strategy
- Onshape basics — they have their wheel CAD files
- Spec constraints (200-305mm length, 45g min mass, 19.5mm cartridge hole, 50-52mm hole depth)

**What this iteration removes:**

- ~3 lessons of physics scaffolding (compress to 1 lesson refresher quiz)
- Sentence-starter fill-in-blanks
- Group work
- "Step X / Tools / Failures / Time" template (replaced by 4-prompt journal)
- Achievement-band rubrics for artifacts (replaced by Three Cs)
- Final-eval-only reflection (replaced by continuous 4-prompt journal)

**What this iteration adds:**

| Concept | Last year | This year |
|---|---|---|
| Agency as goal | Implicit (build a fast car) | **Explicit** — Three Cs rubric, mini-lesson, baseline, calibrations, final reflection |
| Project management | None | **Kanban + WIP=1 + DoD + blockage triage + time-estimation loop** |
| Reflection | 6 prompts at end | **4-prompt journal × 14 classes + 3 self-reread cycles + final reflection** |
| Decision evidence | None | **Three Cs in journal "Decided" slot + survey rubric** |
| Calibration | None | **2 paired self+teacher ratings + 2-min discussion of gap** |
| Pacing awareness | Implicit deadline | **Backward-mapped milestones + Race Day Predictor** |
| Critique | None | **Class 7 decision-critique with Kind/Specific/Helpful protocol** |
| Print pipeline | Direct file handoff | **Preflight** with rule-based scan + Fabricator pickup |

## 3. Pedagogical foundations

### 3.1 The Three Cs — the unit's master rubric

Per Cowork's pedagogical synthesis (5 May 2026), every assessment of agency reduces to three checks:

> **Did the student CHOOSE? Can they explain WHY? Did the choice CHANGE their next action?**

| All 3 present | 2 present | 1 or 0 present |
|---|---|---|
| **Agency demonstrated** | Developing | Compliance / not yet |

This single rubric replaces all of last year's achievement bands. It applies to:
- Journal "Decided" entries (graded inline as you read them)
- Kanban card moves (system can flag cards moved without `because` clause)
- All 5 surveys (students self-rate against the same Three Cs)

Same rubric across self-rating + teacher observation + artifact = clean grading + triangulation.

### 3.2 What counts as evidence of agency (and what doesn't)

Cowork named these specifically:

**Real evidence:**
- *Initiated revision* — changes a wheel design without being told to, and can articulate why (not "it broke" but "the axle hole was off-centre")
- *Calibrated help-seeking* — asks specific questions, not generic ones
- *Trade-off articulation* — names what they gave up to get something else
- *Tool-of-tools choice* — chooses or refuses scaffolds you offered, AND the explanation holds
- *Plan-revision in response to evidence* — updating the plan, not just executing it

**Proxies that LOOK like agency but aren't:**
- Finishing fast (often avoidance, not ownership)
- Polished journal entries (often high conscientiousness + teacher-pleasing)
- Volunteering to help peers (sometimes agency, often social positioning)
- Confident verbal pitches (rehearsed performance ≠ owned thinking)
- Dutiful Kanban use (compliance theatre wearing agency's clothes)

**The diagnostic:** insist on causal *because* clauses ("I chose X *because* Y, even though Z would have been easier"). Refuse vague ones.

### 3.3 Self-Determination Theory framing

Bandura's four-component agentic perspective (intentionality, forethought, self-reactiveness, self-reflectiveness) layered with Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan): agency emerges where **autonomy + competence + relatedness** are simultaneously supported.

At 13-14, the prefrontal cortex is consolidating executive function — making forethought *possible* but not *reliable*. This is the developmental sweet spot for *teaching* agency. Earlier and the metacognitive capacity isn't there; later and compliance habits have set.

### 3.4 The race-day gravity well — must be defended

Cowork's most important strategic warning:

> *"When a hard-dated external deliverable competes with the metacurriculum, the deliverable wins. Students will optimise for the car at the expense of the journal, the kanban, the reflection. Defence: assess agency and PM more visibly than the car. Make the metacurriculum the gradable thing. The race is celebration, not assessment."*

**Concrete:**
- Final grade weighting: journal + Kanban + reflection ≥70%; car ≤30%
- Student-facing dashboard: PM + agency progress is the visible primary surface; car build status is secondary
- Race day = party. Not a test.

Without this, every other design choice degrades. With it, the unit's pedagogy holds.

### 3.5 Cultural posture confound (NIS context)

Cowork's most situationally-relevant warning:

> *"Students from heritage cultures with strong adult-deference norms may rate themselves low not because of low agency but because self-promotion is culturally inappropriate; students fluent in Western self-presentation norms may rate themselves high without owning much."*

**Defence: never let a self-rating stand alone.** Triangulate self + teacher + artifact (Three Cs is naturally evidentiary, which helps). Frame this with students Day 1: *"Your self-rating is one of three signals; the others are what I observe and what your work shows."*

### 3.6 Hidden pitfalls (Cowork's list, ranked by likelihood at NIS)

1. **Compliance theatre** — students learn the language of agency without the substance. Defence: causal *because* clauses, refuse vague ones.
2. **Teacher rescue** — when a student is stuck, the teacher reflex is to help. Productive struggle IS the substrate of agency. Defence: 10-min rule before any teacher intervention; redirect to peer or Kanban first; ask diagnostic questions, not give solutions.
3. **Race-day gravity well** — see §3.4. The most architectural risk.
4. **Cultural posture confound** — see §3.5.
5. **Agency-rich get richer** — high-agency students thrive in open structures, low-agency students drift. Defence: explicit attention rotation toward bottom third (the Teacher Attention-Rotation Panel).
6. **Tool fetish** — Kanban becomes the work, journal becomes the work, platform becomes the work. Defence: name the risk to students Day 1 — *"these tools serve your thinking; when they stop serving it, tell me."*

The "Don't rescue" banner on the teacher dashboard is the daily reconditioning. Students will be stuck. Recovery IS the learning. Safety + dangerous-mistakes only.

## 4. Unit design (v5)

### 4.1 Anchored structure

3 hard anchors, 11 self-paced studio classes:

| Class | Anchor | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Hard anchor** | Unit launch. Agency mini-lesson, Three Cs intro, Strategy Canvas, baseline survey, backward-map milestones, Kanban setup. Sets the curriculum frame. |
| 2-6 | Self-paced studio | Students work through Lessons 2-6 (Ideation → Choose → Working Drawing → Wheel Decision → Transfer to balsa) on their own pacing. Workshop tools available. |
| **7** | **Hard anchor** | Mid-unit critique session. Pairs share one *decision* with Kind/Specific/Helpful feedback. Survey 3 (everyone). Pause point in studio drift. |
| 8-13 | Self-paced studio | Bandsaw → Sand → Print (via Preflight) → Paint → Assemble → Tune. |
| **14** | **Hard anchor** | Race day. Final reflection (deep). Survey 5. Celebration. |

This is closer to a **studio model** than a class-by-class progression. Pedagogically more aggressive than a sequenced version — and right for an agency-focused unit. Echoes NuVu's studio model (Cowork reference).

### 4.2 Per-class envelope (60 min)

**Self-paced studio classes (2-6, 8-13):**
- 5 min open: glance at timeline, pick today's 1 card from "This Class" → move to Doing (WIP=1)
- 45-50 min studio work: workshop / CAD / build / paint
- 8-10 min close: Journal entry (4 prompts + photo) → auto-creates next Kanban card → Kanban update → done

**Anchor classes (1, 7, 14):** see §4.5.

### 4.3 The 11 self-paced lessons (modules, not days)

Lesson modules accessed via Kanban card links, not in fixed order:

| Lesson | Activities (each is a Kanban card) | Dependencies |
|---|---|---|
| 2. Ideation | 3 distinct concept sketches with deep justification per choice | Class 1 done |
| 3. Choose design | Comparison table; Three Cs explicit; declare chosen concept | Lesson 2 |
| 4. Working drawing | 1:1 scale, top + side views, hidden lines for cartridge hole, dimensions, photo upload | Lesson 3 |
| 5. Transfer + drill | Cut paper templates → trace to balsa → 3mm pilot holes | Lesson 4 |
| 6. Wheel decision | **Reuse last year's CAD file OR redesign in Onshape with Three Cs justification** | Independent (do anytime before print) |
| 8. Bandsaw | Profile cut → top view cut → tape waste back together between cuts | Lesson 5 + workshop slot |
| 9. Sand + shape | 80 grit → 220 grit progression; weight check; safety | Lesson 8 |
| 10. Print wheels (via Preflight) | Slice in Bambu, submit via Preflight, await scan + Fabricator pickup | Lesson 6 |
| 11. Paint | Undercoat + finishing coats; weight monitor; dip method optional | Lesson 9 |
| 12. Assembly | Wheels, axles, straws, washers, screw eyes; roll test | Lesson 10 + Lesson 11 |
| 13. Tune | Final adjustments based on roll test; spec-sheet self-check | Lesson 12 |

Plus **support modules** (linked from Kanban cards as needed):
- Physics refresher (Newton's laws + F=ma — 5 min if needed; 0 if confident)
- Spec checklist (the 7 dimensional rules — for self-verification)
- Workshop safety reminders (bandsaw, sander, drill press)

### 4.4 Hard anchors — designed activities

#### Class 1 (~60 min) — Unit launch

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 5 min | **Hook:** 2-clip agency video — same scenario, different responses ("which kid is showing agency?") |
| 5 min | **Co-construct definition** of agency. Surface 3-4 attributes. Pair-share. |
| 5 min | **Three Cs intro** (Choice / Causation / Change) — explicit framing |
| 10 min | **Strategy Canvas** — Design philosophy / Biggest risk / Fallback plan (per student, individual) |
| 10 min | **Backward-map milestones** from June 11 — students drag dots onto timeline from RIGHT to LEFT |
| 10 min | **Kanban setup** — 5 starter cards seeded from common backlog (sketch, drawing, CAD wheel, etc.) |
| 10 min | **Survey 1: Baseline** — 4-question Three Cs self-rating on starting agency |
| 5 min | **Closing:** "What does agency mean for YOU in this unit?" — open journal entry |

#### Class 7 (~60 min) — Mid-unit critique

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 10 min | **Self-reread #1** — read your last 3 journal entries; what's the pattern? Write a 1-paragraph observation. |
| 2 min | **Pair up** by random match (4 pairs + 1 trio for class of 9) |
| 10 min | Each shares ONE decision they made (drawn from Decision Log or a Kanban card's `because` clause) |
| 20 min | **Kind / Specific / Helpful** feedback exchange — receive then give (10 min each direction) |
| 8 min | **Solo write-up:** what feedback will I act on? What card does this become in my Kanban? |
| 10 min | **Survey 3** — Three Cs mid-unit |

#### Class 14 (~60 min) — Race day

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 5 min | Race format reminder + safety briefing |
| 25 min | **The race** — 9 cars, 2 lanes typical, ~3 heats |
| 15 min | **Final reflection** (deep) — side-by-side Survey 1 baseline vs current self-perception. Open prompt: "what changed and why?" |
| 15 min | **Survey 5: Final Three Cs** + open reflection on agency growth (8-10 questions, deeper than mid-unit surveys) |

### 4.5 Calibration conversations (Surveys 2 + 4)

**Survey 2** triggers when student marks "Working drawing complete" on their personal timeline.
**Survey 4** triggers when student marks "Painting complete" on their personal timeline.

Each calibration is **2-min teacher conversation** comparing the student's self-rating to your observation rating on the same Three Cs rubric. Discuss any large gap. **The conversation is where metacognition develops** — not the rating itself.

For class of 9: ~18 min of cumulative calibration time per round. Doable in a single studio class without disrupting other students.

### 4.6 Self-rereading prompts (highest-leverage intervention)

Scheduled at journal entries 5, 9, and 13 (rough — based on student's actual entry count, not class number):

> "Read your last 3 journal entries. What's the pattern? What's surprising about how you've been thinking?"

Becomes a 5th journal field for that entry. Visible to teacher when grading. Used as evidence in the final agency reflection (Class 14).

### 4.7 Journal — 4-prompt structure

Each entry, ~1.5-2 min to fill:

| # | Prompt | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Did** — what you actually did, not what you planned | Anti-fiction anchor (the gap between plan and reality is itself evidence) |
| 2 | **Noticed** — what surprised you, broke, or didn't go as expected | Engineering the doorway to learning. Schön / van Es / Berger. Without this, journals collapse to chronicles. |
| 3 | **Decided** — what you changed your mind about, with a *because* clause 🌱 NM | Three Cs anchor. Causal because mandatory. |
| 4 | **Next** — your next move, and why *that* and not something else | Auto-creates Kanban card with the "why-not-Y" buried in card metadata |

**Photo-of-decision** required (not photo-of-work). Forces concrete framing per Reggio Emilia's documentation pedagogy.

Optional 5th: **"Stuck on anything?"** (only renders if checked) → routes to blockage triage.

Send-to-portfolio button → captures via existing portfolio infra (audit pending).

### 4.8 Surveys — 5 ratings, uneven cadence

Per Cowork's Experience-Sampling-Method literature: 14 surveys = noise, 3 = sparse. **5 ratings, uneven cadence:**

| # | When | Type |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Class 1 (anchored) | **Baseline** — full Three Cs self-rate |
| 2 | When student completes "Working drawing" milestone | + **paired teacher rating + 2-min calibration conversation** |
| 3 | Class 7 (anchored) | **Mid-unit** — Three Cs |
| 4 | When student completes "Painting" milestone | + **paired teacher rating + 2-min calibration conversation** |
| 5 | Class 14 (anchored) | **Final reflection** (deep — 8-10 questions, side-by-side with baseline) |

**Plus daily 1-slider micro-check:** "How focused were you today? 1-10" — 5 seconds, end of every class. Pure momentum tracking, not NM rubric. No fatigue risk.

### 4.9 Kanban — the unit's actual scheduler

Each card has:

| Field | Required | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Title | Yes | Short imperative ("Cut profile on bandsaw") |
| Lesson activity link | Auto | Click card → opens the relevant lesson activity |
| Definition of Done | Yes (only on cards in "This Class") | "I'll know this is done when..." |
| Time estimate | When moved to Doing | minutes |
| Time actual | When moved to Done | auto-filled from timestamp |
| Blocked? | Optional | If true → blockage triage flow |
| Because clause | Required when moved to Done | Three Cs evidence |

**Columns:**

```
┌─────────────┬─────────────┬─────────────┬─────────────┐
│  BACKLOG    │  THIS CLASS │  DOING      │  DONE       │
│  (10+ ok)   │  (3-5 max)  │  (1 max!) ⚡│  (∞)        │
├─────────────┼─────────────┼─────────────┼─────────────┤
│ Sketch 3    │ Cut profile │ Sand bottom │ Mass = 65g  │
│ Mass test   │ Mass check  │ ⚠ blocked   │ Sketches    │
│ Wheel CAD   │             │             │             │
└─────────────┴─────────────┴─────────────┴─────────────┘
```

**WIP limit on Doing = 1** (per Cowork). Sell as "finish before you start."

**Blockage triage** — tap any card → "I'm stuck → why?" → 4-button: Tool / Skill / Decision / Help. Each routes to a different scaffold:
- Tool → "Have you checked the workshop tool checkout?"
- Skill → "Three classmates have done this — see who's free"
- Decision → "Pick one option for now. You can iterate."
- Help → pings teacher with snapshot of where they're stuck

**Time-estimation loop:** estimate when moving to Doing, actual auto-tracked. After 6 cards, show running estimate-vs-actual ratio. One of the highest-leverage agency-adjacent meta-skills available in PM.

### 4.10 Timeline — backward-map + forward-plan

**Day 1 backward-mapping mode:** Race Day (Jun 11) fixed on right. Students drag milestones from RIGHT TO LEFT in reverse order: Race ← Painted ← Assembled ← Printed ← CAD modeled ← Shaped ← Drawn ← Designed.

Each placement asks: "If I want this done by [date], when must the previous step finish?"

**Daily forward-planning mode:** "What 2 things must move to Done today to stay on track against your milestone map?"

Same data model, two interaction modes triggered by context.

**Spec compliance as DoD seeds:** the 7 spec rules from last year's PPTs become Definition-of-Done criteria on the relevant Kanban cards. Spec compliance becomes a self-managed gate, not a teacher gate:

| Spec | Min | Max |
|---|---|---|
| Body length | 200 mm | 305 mm |
| Body mass (with wheels) | 45 g | — |
| Power plant hole depth | 50 mm | 52 mm |
| Power plant housing thickness | 3 mm | — |
| Power plant housing diameter | 19.5 mm | 3/4" |
| Lowest chamber to race surface | 26 mm | 36 mm |
| Screw eye inside diameter | 4 mm | — |

### 4.11 Strategy Canvas (Class 1)

Single page, 3 boxes per student:

- **My car's design philosophy** (1 line — "low and aerodynamic" / "wide and stable" / "lightweight at all costs")
- **My biggest risk** (1 line — "running out of time on print" / "wheels won't align")
- **My fallback plan** (1 line — "if X happens, I'll Y")

Saved. Shown back at start of every studio class as a header strip on timeline view. Re-prompt at Class 7: "anything changed?"

### 4.12 Decision Log (optional support tool)

Quick voice-or-text logger:
> "I just decided to use balsa instead of basswood because basswood is too heavy."

Tap-to-record, 30 seconds. Builds up as a chronological feed. Used as evidence in the final agency reflection (Class 14). Also gives Matt scrollable insight when grading NM.

### 4.13 Race Day Predictor (Class 6+ optional)

Student inputs current state weekly:
- Design % complete (slider)
- Build % complete (slider)
- Test runs done (count)

Output: visual readiness chip + projection. *"You'll be race-ready by June 11 ✅"* / *"Tight — drop one stretch goal"* / *"Behind — let's regroup."*

Pure motivation/calibration tool, not assessment. Build at Class 6, use Classes 7 onward.

### 4.14 Teacher dashboard — Attention-Rotation Panel

```
┌─ ATTENTION ROTATION ─────────────────┐
│  Bottom third by Three Cs score:      │
│  • Sara — last seen Class 6 (3 cls)  │
│  • Alex — last 1:1 Class 4           │
│  • Mei  — no Decided entry x 3 cls   │
│                                       │
│  Suggested 1:1 today: Sara            │
└───────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Surfaces:**
- Bottom third by Three Cs aggregate
- Last journal entry timestamp per student
- Last Kanban card move per student
- Last calibration conversation per student
- Suggested 1:1 today

Without this, you'll over-attend the engaged students. Cowork's "agency-rich get richer" pitfall.

### 4.15 Don't Rescue banner (teacher-facing only)

Persistent banner at top of teacher's unit dashboard:

```
🛑 Don't rescue. Recovery IS the learning.
   (Safety + dangerous-mistakes only)
```

You'll see it 14 times. Probably enough to recondition the impulse to fix mistakes mid-class.

### 4.16 Preflight integration

All 3D printing routes through **Preflight** (the fabrication submission pipeline shipped earlier in this development cycle). For students:

1. Slice their wheel CAD file in Bambu Studio
2. Submit through StudioLoom → Preflight scan (rule-based STL checks)
3. Lab tech (Fabricator role) processes the print
4. Student notified on pickup

This is the **first real classroom deployment of Preflight** — 9 students × 1-2 prints each = ~10-15 jobs through the queue. Manageable smoke test that doubles as authentic PM constraint (students learn that "I'll print tomorrow" depends on lab tech availability + queue).

## 8. Provenance

This document was drafted 5 May 2026 over a single development session as part of the StudioLoom unit-design pivot. Inputs:

1. Matt's classroom context + pedagogical framing
2. Last year's CO2 Racers PPTs (6 files)
3. Three lenses of synthesis: Claude (lead), Gemini, Cowork (sequential)
4. Iteration through v1 → v2 → v3 → v4 → v5 across the conversation

The unit design pivot point: realizing that agency-as-curriculum requires the metacurriculum to outweigh the artifact in grading — Cowork's "race-day gravity well" insight. Without it, the unit becomes "we built cars and also did some journaling."

The implementation pivot point: realizing that "students choose order and timing" is closer to a studio model than a sequenced class progression, requiring the Kanban to become the actual scheduler with lesson-activity links — not a sidebar tool.

Document maintained as the foundation reference for the unit. Updates logged inline with date stamps.
