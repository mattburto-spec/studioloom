Connections the "Boring Lists" Reveal

HOTTEST MATCH Dairy Powder → Infant Formula. ChAFTA just eliminated the 10% tariff on milk powders (Jan 2026). MOFCOM's 2025 catalogue encourages foreign investment in dairy processing. China's infant formula market is growing from US$19.6B to $36.2B. GACC Decree 280 (June 1) creates a new registration barrier — exporters who comply early get a moat. Three signals from three different "boring lists" all pointing at the same opportunity.
HOTTEST MATCH Barley → Craft Brewing. Anti-dumping duties removed Aug 2023. MOFCOM 2025 catalogue explicitly encourages "raw materials for beer brewing" as a foreign investment category. Australian barley exports already hit US$1.22B in 2024. But here's the non-obvious bit: Chinese craft breweries are booming and they want premium malting barley — not just feed grade. An Australian maltster connected to a Nanjing craft brewery = a deal nobody's making because nobody reads both lists.
DEADLINE GACC Decree 280 — June 1, 2026. Every Australian food facility exporting to China needs to re-register under new rules. Many don't know yet. This is a compliance bottleneck that creates consulting revenue for anyone who can explain it clearly. The DAFF list of registered establishments cross-referenced with the GACC approved facility register will show exactly which Australian exporters are at risk of losing market access.
NEW SIGNAL Health Supplements — AU brands trusted, CN demand surging. China's health food imports up 7.2% in 2025. Australian brands (Blackmores, Swisse) already have trust. But NMPA registration is complex and most smaller AU supplement makers don't attempt it. Cross-reference NMPA approved products with Australian supplement manufacturers → find who's NOT registered but could be. That gap = a consulting opportunity.
GAP Wine — back in the game but market has changed. Tariffs removed in 2024 after 4 years. AU wine flooding back (A$893M). But the Chinese wine market is now 1/3 of its 2019 peak. The opportunity isn't just "export more wine" — it's helping wineries understand that the market has fundamentally changed. Different channels (Douyin live-streaming vs. traditional distributors), different price points, different consumer. Someone who knows both sides could charge for market re-entry strategy.
GAP Cold Chain — the infrastructure opportunity nobody talks about. China loses ~30% of perishable food in transit. Massive government investment in cold chain infrastructure. This isn't a product match — it's a service match. Australian cold chain technology and expertise could be sold to Chinese logistics companies. Cross-reference AusTender cold chain projects with Chinese provincial procurement portals → find where the infrastructure money is flowing.
