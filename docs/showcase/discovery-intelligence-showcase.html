🧱 Architecture

Three modules, not eight stations

Under the hood, the 8 stations are architecturally three composable modules — each with its own lifecycle, refresh cadence, and data output.

🪞

Identity

"Who are you as a designer?"

Archetype scores, working style vector, interest map, irritation signals. The stable core of who a student is.

⏱️ Done well once, rarely repeated. Confirmed or adjusted on return visits.
🔍

Problem Finding

"What matters to you right now?"

Problem statement, resource inventory, confidence profile. Context-specific to each unit.

⏱️ Fresh each unit. Gets smarter with project history context.
🎯

Commitment

"What are you going to do about it?"

Chosen direction, fear areas, project brief, success criteria. Always specific to the project ahead.

⏱️ Always fresh — specific to each new project.

🌐 Frameworks

Same engine, different journeys

Discovery adapts its tone, scope, and project framing to the IB programme context — from 6-week MYP Design units to year-long Personal Projects.

MYP Design

Mode 1 — Design Units

Students complete guided lessons first, teacher unlocks Discovery, then Open Studio. Doors are 6-10 week project scale, Kit references MYP criteria.

Kit's tone: Encouraging workshop buddy
Service as Action

Mode 2 — Service Projects

Discovery IS the unit entry point — no prerequisites. Module 2 emphasises community needs and service orientation. Doors frame real-impact service projects.

Kit's tone: Empathy and social responsibility
Personal Project

Year-Long Commitment

Highest stakes. Module 2 is deeper and more personal. Doors are year-long commitments, not 8-week sprints. Additional milestone decomposition station.

Kit: "This is a big decision. Let's make sure it's something you'll still care about in March."
PYP Exhibition

Collaborative Discovery

Individual profiles compared to form complementary teams. A team of all Makers with no Researcher will struggle with inquiry. Group-aware project doors.

Kit: "Your team has strong Makers but no one who loves research. How will you handle the inquiry phase?"
🗺️ Roadmap

Four phases to full intelligence

Each phase builds on the last. Phase A delivers immediate value with zero new infrastructure. Full intelligence accumulates over the school year.

🅰️

Profile-Aware Design Assistant

~2-3 days · Zero new dependencies

Inject Discovery profile into the AI mentor's system prompt. The Design Assistant immediately knows: archetype, working style, fear areas, interests, self-efficacy gaps. Every conversation becomes personalised. A student who fears public speaking gets gentler critique scaffolding. A Maker gets hands-on examples instead of theory.

🅱️

Adaptive Discovery Flow

~3-4 days · Requires Phase A

Discovery gets shorter and smarter on every return visit. "Confirm & Adjust" UI for returning students. Kit references previous projects and growth. First-time students get the full 45-minute journey; veterans get 10 minutes of context-specific problem finding and project selection.

🅲

Persistent Profile + Passive Signals

~5-7 days · Requires Phase B

Canonical student profile table accumulates across sessions. Background jobs aggregate toolkit usage, pace data, and conversation patterns. Teacher dashboard shows class-wide archetype distribution and common growth areas. AI lesson generation uses class profile data.

🅳

Framework Variants + Group Discovery

~5-8 days · Requires Phase C

Personal Project variant with year-long commitment framing and milestone decomposition. PYP Exhibition variant with group profiling and complementary team formation. First-time onboarding flow with avatar selection. Teacher content control panel for Discovery content pools.

🛡️ Ethics

Built on student agency

Every design decision puts students in control. This isn't surveillance — it's self-reflection that happens to make AI smarter.

✋

Student Agency

The profile is built through deliberate self-reflection, not surveillance. Students choose what to share.

👁️

Full Transparency

Students can see their own profile at any time. Nothing is hidden. Teachers see profiles to inform instruction.

🔄

Self-Report First

Passive signals enrich but never override self-reported identity. A student who says they're a Maker is a Maker.

🔒

Private Profiles

No class rankings, no public comparison, no "you're behind" messaging. Profiles are visible to the student and their teachers only.

🔁

Reset Available

Students can request a full profile reset at any time. Teachers can flag sections for refresh.

🧒

Age-Appropriate

Designed for ages 11-18. Archetypes are tendencies, not categories. No personality "typing" that feels reductive.
