# StudioLoom — Mentor Character Model Prompts

**Style reference:** *Another World* (Eric Chahi, 1991)
**Universal rules:** Flat shaded, no textures, no face detail, 100–300 triangles, 2–4 solid colours per figure, identity through silhouette and posture only.

**Usage:** These prompts work for AI mesh generation (Meshy, Tripo3D, Rodin) or as written briefs for a Blender artist. For AI generation, prefix each with: *"Low polygon flat shaded 3D character, no face, no textures, solid colour materials, Another World 1991 video game art style."*

---

## 1. I.M. Pei

**Silhouette:** Tall, upright, slender. Perfectly vertical posture suggesting quiet authority. Broad angular shoulders under a structured jacket. One arm extended forward at waist height with open palm — presenting an invisible object or idea to the viewer. The other arm at his side. Head slightly lifted, looking forward and upward.

**Clothing shape:** Clean-cut suit jacket with geometric shoulder line. Simple trousers with a straight fall. No wrinkles, no folds — the clothing is as precise as the architecture. Round glasses suggested by two small circular polygon cutouts or a thin bar across the face area (the only "face" detail — and it's geometric, not expressive).

**Pose essence:** A man showing you something he believes in. Confident but not aggressive. The presenting hand is the focal point.

**Colours:**
- Body/suit: cool blue-grey (#4a6274)
- Shirt/chest accent: lighter grey (#8a9aaa)
- Highlight edges: pale blue (#b8c8d8)

**Polygon budget:** 150–200

## 2. Zaha Hadid

**Silhouette:** Dynamic, leaning into motion. A dramatic flowing coat or cape that extends behind and to one side — the fabric doesn't hang straight, it sweeps in a curve like one of her buildings. One leg forward mid-stride. The body leans forward at maybe 5 degrees. Head held high, chin up. Arms obscured by the coat's volume.

**Clothing shape:** The coat is the character. It's oversized and sculptural — wider than the body, with angular folds that suggest movement frozen in time. Beneath it, the body is relatively simple. The coat's hem is asymmetric, longer on one side. Think of the figure as a walking building.

**Pose essence:** Someone who has somewhere important to be and won't slow down. Unstoppable forward momentum.

**Colours:**
- Coat: warm sand (#c9a87c)
- Body beneath: dark charcoal (#2a2a2a)
- Coat highlight/edge: pale gold (#e8d8c0)

**Polygon budget:** 180–250 (the coat geometry needs more faces)

## 3. Tadao Ando

**Silhouette:** The simplest figure in the set. Almost rectangular. Standing perfectly centred, perfectly still, feet together, arms at sides. The body is a near-geometric block — like a column of concrete. Shoulders are square but not broad. The head is a simple rounded form sitting directly on the shoulders with minimal neck.

**Clothing shape:** Impossible to tell where the clothing ends and the body begins. A simple high-collared jacket merges with trousers into one continuous vertical form. No belt, no lapels, no detail. The figure IS a column.

**Pose essence:** Absolute stillness. Meditation. The kind of person who stands in a room and listens to the silence. The least amount of geometry in the entire roster — and that's the point.

**Colours:**
- Entire body: raw concrete grey (#7a7a72)
- Subtle shadow on lower half: darker grey (#5a5a54)
- No highlight colour — the rim light in the scene provides the only edge definition

**Polygon budget:** 100–150 (deliberately the lowest)

## 4. Diébédo Francis Kéré

**Silhouette:** Wide, grounded stance. Feet planted apart, weight distributed evenly — this person is connected to the earth. Arms slightly open at the sides, palms facing forward at hip height — a welcoming, open gesture. Shoulders broad and relaxed, not tense. The overall shape is a gentle triangle: wide at the base, tapering to the head.

**Clothing shape:** A loose, comfortable tunic or shirt that falls to mid-thigh over simple trousers. The tunic has a slight A-line flare that reinforces the grounded triangle silhouette. No sharp tailoring — the clothing breathes. A subtle raised collar or neckline detail.

**Pose essence:** Someone who makes space for you. Open arms, open stance, open to whatever you bring. Warmth communicated through geometry alone.

**Colours:**
- Tunic: warm terracotta (#c0805a)
- Trousers/lower: deeper earth brown (#7a5240)
- Accent: sun-baked clay highlight (#d4a574)

**Polygon budget:** 150–200

## 5. Luis Barragán

**Silhouette:** Medium build, standing in a slight contrapposto — one hip shifted, one shoulder slightly higher. One hand raised and resting flat against an invisible wall beside him (the wall is implied, not modelled — the hand gesture alone suggests it). The other arm hangs loosely. Head turned slightly to one side as if listening to the colour of a room.

**Clothing shape:** A simple blazer over an open-collared shirt. The blazer is boxy and mid-century in proportion — not slim-fit, not oversized. Clean but not rigid. The trousers are straight and simple.

**Pose essence:** A person standing in a courtyard at golden hour, hand on a warm pink wall, listening to silence. Contemplative but sensual — aware of the physical world around him.

**Colours:**
- Blazer: warm neutral (#8a7a6a)
- Shirt/chest: cream white (#e8ddd0)
- Accent polygon (the "wall-touching" hand or one edge): vivid Barragán pink (#e05297)

**Polygon budget:** 120–180

## 6. Dieter Rams

**Silhouette:** Tall, thin, precise. Standing straight with a very slight forward lean — not aggressive, just attentive. Arms close to the body. One hand is raised to chin height, holding or examining a small rectangular object (a product, a detail). The other arm supports the examining elbow. Classic "studying something closely" pose.

**Clothing shape:** A simple turtleneck sweater and trousers. No jacket, no layers — reductive. The turtleneck creates a clean cylinder from neck to waist. Everything is vertical and parallel. The clothing says "I have eliminated all unnecessary elements from my wardrobe."

**Pose essence:** Total focus on a small object. The world could be on fire and this person would still be examining whether this radius is 1mm too wide.

**Colours:**
- Turtleneck/body: clean white (#e8e8e8)
- Trousers: medium grey (#888888)
- The small object in hand: Braun-inspired dark (#2a2a2a)

**Polygon budget:** 100–150

## 7. Charlotte Perriand

One leg folded, one slightly extended. Body leaning forward with interest. One hand resting on a knee, the other gesturing gently — mid-conversation.

**Clothing shape:** Practical and unfussy. A simple blouse or work shirt with rolled sleeves. Relaxed trousers or a skirt with natural drape. The clothing moves with the body, not against it. A slight scarf or neckerchief adds one touch of personality.

**Pose essence:** Someone in the middle of a conversation with a craftsperson in a mountain workshop. Curious, engaged, leaning in. The seated pose is unique in the roster and makes her immediately identifiable.

**Colours:**
- Blouse: warm linen (#c8b898)
- Trousers/skirt: deeper olive-brown (#6a7a5a)
- Scarf accent: muted red (#a05040)

## 8. Hella Jongerius

**Pose essence:** Hands at work. Always making, always touching material. This person thinks with their fingers. The apron immediately reads as "maker."

**Colours:**

## 9. Jony Ive

**Silhouette:** Clean, medium build. Standing with both hands held at chest height, cupped around a small object — holding it with extraordinary care, as if it were made of spun glass. Head tilted very slightly downward, looking at the object in his hands. Feet together. Body perfectly still.

**Clothing shape:** A plain crew-neck t-shirt and simple trousers. The most basic possible clothing — the person disappears, the object in the hands is everything. No collar, no jacket, no accessories. The t-shirt is fitted, not baggy.

**Pose essence:** Reverence for a small thing. The entire world has contracted to the object in this person's hands. Everything else has fallen away.

**Colours:**
- T-shirt: silver-grey (#a0a0a8)

- The held object: a subtle lighter highlight (#c8c8d0) — the brightest point on the figure

## 10. Yinka Ilori

One hand raised at shoulder height with an open palm — a storytelling gesture, mid-sentence. The other hand at the hip or resting on the thigh. Head slightly forward, engaged, animated.

**Clothing shape:** A patterned or colour-blocked jacket or bomber over a simple base. The jacket has two or three distinct colour zones, broken into geometric panels — this is the one character where the clothing itself uses multiple colours as a deliberate design statement. Relaxed trousers. Trainers or chunky shoes giving a slightly wider base.

**Pose essence:** The friend who's telling you an incredible story and using their whole body to do it. Joy and energy in every angle. The most dynamic standing pose in the roster.

**Colours:**

- Jacket panel 2: golden yellow (#fdcb6e)
- Trousers: deep navy or black (#1a1a2e)

## 11. Yohji Yamamoto

**Silhouette:** The most asymmetric figure. One shoulder higher than the other. Draped fabric hangs from the body at uneven lengths — a long coat or wrap that's longer on the left side than the right. The body beneath is obscured, almost lost in cloth. A slight slouch, weight on one leg. Head slightly bowed.

**Clothing shape:** Layers of dark fabric. An oversized coat or wrap with raw, unfinished-looking edges (achieved with irregular polygon outlines, not smooth curves). The coat hangs open, revealing a darker layer beneath. Nothing is fitted, nothing is symmetrical. The clothing overwhelms the body — intentionally.

**Pose essence:** A shadow. Someone who has made darkness beautiful. The asymmetry is the identity — remove it and the character becomes generic.

**Colours:**
- Outer layer: pure black (#1a1a1a)
- Inner layer: slightly lighter black (#2d2d2d)
- One single edge highlight: deep charcoal blue (#2a2a3a) — barely visible, only catching rim light

**Polygon budget:** 180–250 (the draped fabric layers need the extra geometry)

## 12. Iris van Herpen

**Silhouette:** The most elaborate silhouette. Standing upright and still, but the clothing extends outward from the body in organic, almost coral-like or skeletal forms. Think of a human figure with sculptural elements growing from the shoulders, hips, or spine — not wings, more like natural crystal formations or branching structures. The body is slim and vertical; the extensions are the spectacle.

**Clothing shape:** A fitted bodysuit or dress as the base form, with 3–5 angular protrusions extending outward at irregular angles. These aren't smooth curves — they're faceted, crystalline, geometric interpretations of organic growth. They should look like they're mid-formation, still growing. Each protrusion is 3–6 polygons.

**Pose essence:** A human becoming something else. Evolution frozen mid-process. Standing still but radiating outward energy through the sculptural extensions.

**Colours:**
- Body base: cool violet (#7a6890)
- Extensions: lighter lavender (#b8b0d0)
- Edge highlight: pale iridescent (#d8d0e8)

**Polygon budget:** 200–300 (the extensions justify the highest budget in the roster)

## 13. Paula Scher

**Silhouette:** Strong, planted stance. Hands on hips, elbows out — creating a bold, wide upper-body silhouette. Or one arm extended outward and upward, pointing or directing at something large and off-screen. Feet shoulder-width apart. Chin up. The body language says "LOOK AT THIS."

**Clothing shape:** A bold, structured jacket with angular shoulders. The jacket is boxy and strong, not flowing. Underneath, a simple dark top. Trousers are straight and grounded. The jacket's geometry is the most angular in the roster — sharp corners, no soft curves.

**Pose essence:** The creative director pointing at a wall-sized typographic installation. Commanding the room. Energy directed outward at massive scale. Hands-on-hips or pointing — both work as long as the silhouette is WIDE and LOUD.

**Colours:**
- Jacket: bold red (#d03030)
- Base clothing: black (#1a1a1a)
- One accent edge: white (#e8e8e8)

**Polygon budget:** 150–200

## 14. Kenya Hara

**Silhouette:** The most minimal human form possible. Almost a rectangle with a head. Standing perfectly centred, arms at sides, feet together, body almost featureless. Less detailed than even Ando — approaching pure abstraction. The figure should almost dissolve into the background, especially in the white/bright lighting preset.

**Clothing shape:** Impossible to identify specific garments. The entire figure is one continuous vertical form. No collar, no hem lines, no seams. A human-shaped column of white. The only articulation is the separation between head and body, and a subtle narrowing at the waist.

**Pose essence:** Presence through absence. The figure is there but barely — like a shadow cast by nothing, or a space where a person used to stand. The viewer should feel that the emptiness around the figure is more important than the figure itself.

**Colours:**
- Entire figure: warm white (#ede8e0)
- Subtle shadow on one side: barely darker (#ddd8d0)
- No accent colour. The figure is almost monochrome.

**Polygon budget:** 80–120 (the fewest in the roster — intentionally)

## 16. Charles & Ray Eames

**Silhouette:** TWO FIGURES — the only pair in the roster. One taller (Charles), one slightly shorter (Ray), standing side by side with a small gap between them. The taller figure has one arm extended, pointing at something. The shorter figure is turned slightly toward the taller one, engaged. Their body language is collaborative — they're looking at the same thing.

**Clothing shape:** The taller figure wears a simple jacket and tie (mid-century casual). The shorter figure wears a dress or skirt with a slightly flared hem — the skirt's A-line creates a distinctive triangular lower silhouette that immediately reads as a different person. Both wear simple shoes.

**Pose essence:** Two people looking at the same problem and delighting in it together. The pointing arm is key — it directs attention outward, to the work, not to themselves. They are always in service of the idea.

**Colours:**
- Charles: warm brown jacket (#6a4a30) with cream shirt (#d8d0c0)
- Ray: deeper red-brown dress (#8a3a30) with lighter accent (#c08070)
- Both share the same shoe colour to ground them as a unit

**Polygon budget:** 200–300 (split across both figures, ~125–150 each)

## 18. Neri Oxman

**Silhouette:** Upright figure with arms slightly raised from the sides, palms up — a presenting or receiving gesture. The clothing or body has organic extensions or irregularities that suggest biological growth. Not as extreme as van Herpen's crystalline extensions, but the edges of the figure are slightly irregular, as if the body is mid-transformation. Think of the boundary between the figure and the air being fuzzy, porous.

**Clothing shape:** A fitted base layer with 2–3 organic protrusions or thickenings at the shoulders, forearms, or hips — like chitin forming, or bark growing. These aren't garments — they're material becoming something. Simpler than van Herpen but sharing the "growing" quality.

**Pose essence:** A scientist-artist holding their hands open to nature, receiving and giving simultaneously. The slightly raised arms with palms up suggest both offering and collecting. The organic irregularities on the body suggest she IS the material ecology she studies.

**Colours:**
- Body base: warm amber (#a09060)
- Organic growths: darker amber-brown (#706040)
- Edge highlight: golden (#c8b070)

**Polygon budget:** 200–300

## 20. Nendo (Oki Sato)

**Silhouette:** Small, slight build. The most delicate figure. Standing with a subtle head tilt — 5–10 degrees to one side, as if examining something small and fascinating on the ground or on a table. One hand raised to chin or cheek in a thinking gesture. The other hand at the side. Body slightly turned, not fully facing the viewer — a 15-degree rotation suggesting a private moment of observation.

**Clothing shape:** The simplest, most unremarkable clothing possible. A plain shirt and trousers with no distinguishing features. The clothing should be so neutral it almost disappears — the head tilt and hand gesture ARE the entire identity. Round glasses suggested by a thin bar or two small circles (similar to Pei but smaller, more delicate).

**Pose essence:** Someone who just noticed that a shadow on the floor looks like a sleeping cat, and is quietly delighted by it. The smallest gesture, the smallest surprise. If you walked past this person on the street you might not notice them — but they've noticed everything about you.

**Colours:**
- Entire figure: soft warm beige (#d0c4b0)
- Subtle trouser differentiation: slightly darker (#b8ae9a)
- Glasses detail: thin dark (#4a4a4a)

**Polygon budget:** 100–150

## Batch Generation Notes

### For AI mesh tools (Meshy / Tripo3D / Rodin)

Prefix every prompt with:

> "Low polygon 3D character in the art style of the 1991 video game Another World by Eric Chahi. Flat shaded polygons, no smooth shading, no textures, solid colour faces only. Faceless — no eyes, mouth, or facial features. Maximum 200 polygons. Single static pose."

Then append the character-specific description above.

**Post-generation cleanup in Blender:**
1. Decimate to target polygon count
2. Delete all textures and UV maps
3. Assign 2–4 solid-colour materials per face groups
4. Set to Shade Flat
5. Run silhouette check (render as pure black from 4 angles)
6. Export .glb with Draco compression

### For commissioning a Blender artist

Send this entire document as the brief. Include:
- The Another World screenshot reference (Church of the Light scene, the intro running sequence, the swimming scene — all show the flat-shaded character style)
- The colour hex codes for each character
- The polygon budget per character
- The silhouette test requirement (must be identifiable as black silhouette)
- Export specs: .glb, Draco compression, origin at feet, all transforms applied
