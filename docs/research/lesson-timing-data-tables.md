## TABLE 1: Standard Period Structures with Research-Backed Timings

### 45-Minute Period (Tight)
| Component | Duration | % of Period | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------------|-------|
| Opening/Hook | 3–5 min | 7–11% | Engage interest, set context |
| Mini-Lesson | 8–10 min | 18–22% | Direct instruction (rule: max 1 + student age) |
| Work Time | 25–30 min | 56–67% | Independent/group making |
| Debrief/Closing | 2–5 min | 4–11% | Consolidate, preview next |
| **Total** | **45 min** | **100%** | Feels rushed for design work |

**Teacher feedback:** "Race against the clock"; suboptimal for sustained making.

---

### 60-Minute Period (Standard Secondary)
| Component | Duration | % of Period | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------------|-------|
| Opening/Warm-up | 5 min | 8% | Attendance, review, context |
| Mini-Lesson | 10–15 min | 17–25% | Teach skill, demo technique |
| Work Time | 35–40 min | 58–67% | Main learning; teacher circulates |
| Debrief/Reflection | 5–10 min | 8–17% | Peer share, exit ticket, next steps |
| **Total** | **60 min** | **100%** | Good for self-paced work + conferencing |

**Optimal configuration:** Warm-up (5) + Mini-Lesson (12) + Work Time (38) + Debrief (5) = 60 min

---

### 90-Minute Block (Extended/Double Period)

#### Variant A: Instruction-Heavy (For complex skill introduction)
| Component | Duration | % of Block | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Opening | 5 min | 5% | Hook, objectives |
| Mini-Lesson 1 | 20–25 min | 22–28% | Teach primary concept |
| Work Time (Part 1) | 20–25 min | 22–28% | Guided practice, scaffolded |
| Check-in/Refocus | 5 min | 5% | Reset, questions, momentum |
| Work Time (Part 2) | 25–30 min | 28–33% | More independent work |
| Closing | 5–10 min | 5–11% | Reflection, exit ticket |
| **Total** | **90 min** | **100%** | Whole-group instruction caps at 30 min |

**Research note:** One secondary math teacher found 30 min direct instruction is the max before adolescent engagement drops. Structure breaks instruction into 2 chunks with a refocus point.

#### Variant B: Hands-On-Heavy (For making/design emphasis)
| Component | Duration | % of Block | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Opening | 5 min | 5% | Context, challenge, resources overview |
| Mini-Lesson | 10–15 min | 11–17% | Teach essential skill only |
| Extended Work Time | 60–65 min | 67–72% | Main event; teacher rotating conferences |
| Check-ins | 2×5 min @ 30/60 min | Built into work | Refocus points for large projects |
| Closing | 5–10 min | 5–11% | Reflection, cleanup, next steps |
| **Total** | **90 min** | **100%** | Best for design, making, sustained flow |

**Planning strategy:** Mark 30-min and 60-min check-in points on the lesson plan. Use these to refocus cohorts, troubleshoot, or pivot if needed.

## TABLE 2: Within-Lesson Timing by Design Phase

### Single-Lesson Ideation (SCAMPER, Brainstorm, Reverse Brainstorm)
| Phase | Duration | Details |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Intro** | 5–8 min | Explain tool, challenge, example |
| **Setup** (if group) | 2–3 min | Teams form, materials ready |
| **Ideation Work** | 20–25 min | Students generate ideas (AI prompts unlocked after first idea) |
| **Idea Sharing** | 5 min | Brief peer share (optional) |
| **AI Synthesis** | 3–5 min | AI nudge, summary of patterns |
| **Reflection** | 2–3 min | Exit ticket, next steps |
| **Total (in a 60-min period)** | **37–47 min** | Leaves 13–23 min flex or transition |

---

### Single-Lesson Evaluation (PMI, Decision Matrix, SWOT)
| Phase | Duration | Details |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Intro** | 5 min | Frame decision/options, explain grid |
| **Column/Quadrant Setup** | 5–8 min | Add options/criteria (students define) |
| **Scoring & Reasoning** | 15–20 min | Fill grid cells + justify (AI challenges reasoning) |
| **Analysis** | 5–10 min | Identify trade-offs, patterns |
| **Decision/Recommendation** | 3–5 min | What does the data suggest? |
| **Reflection** | 2–3 min | What did we learn about this decision? |
| **Total (in a 60-min period)** | **35–51 min** | Leaves 9–25 min flex |

---

### Single-Lesson Analysis (Five Whys, Empathy Map, Affinity Diagram)
| Phase | Duration | Details |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Intro** | 5–8 min | Frame the subject, explain process, example |
| **Step 1** | 3–5 min | Brainstorm/input (student writes, AI listens) |
| **Steps 2–5** (or Quadrants) | 3–5 min each = 12–20 min | Deepen/expand (previous answer visible as context) |
| **Pattern Recognition** | 5 min | What's the root cause / core insight? |
| **Synthesis** | 3–5 min | AI-generated summary + implications |
| **Reflection** | 2–3 min | How does this change your thinking? |
| **Total (in a 60-min period)** | **35–49 min** | Leaves 11–25 min flex |

## TABLE 3: Multi-Lesson Design Unit Pacing (6–8 Week Half-Term)

### 6-Week Unit, 2 Periods/Week (12 total periods)

| Week | Theme | Period 1 | Period 2 | Cumulative | Notes |
|------|-------|---------|---------|-----------|-------|
| **1** | Inquiry & Problem Definition | Mini-lesson: design brief | Research work time | 2 periods | Students gather context, define problem |
| **2** | Research & Analysis | Analysis work (data synthesis) | Empathy mapping / stakeholder analysis | 4 periods | Document findings; 4-5 data points expected |
| **3** | Ideation | Mini-lesson: ideation tool | Brainstorm/SCAMPER work | 6 periods | Generate 20+ ideas; refine to 3–5 promising |
| **4** | Concept Development | Quick evaluation (PMI or similar) | Concept sketching/iteration | 8 periods | Select direction; develop detailed concept |
| **5** | Prototyping & Testing | Build/prototype work | Test & iterate | 10 periods | First prototype; gather feedback |
| **6** | Refinement & Reflection | Refine prototype | Reflection, documentation, exhibition prep | 12 periods | Final iteration; prepare portfolio/showcase |

**Flexibility points:**
- If Week 2 research runs long, extend to Week 2–3 (move ideation to Week 4).
- If Week 5 prototyping reveals major design flaw, students can do second iteration (Week 5.5).
- Early finishers in Week 4: build second prototype variation OR interview additional stakeholders OR analyze competitor products.

---

### 8-Week Unit, 2.5 Periods/Week (20 total periods)

| Week | Focus | Periods Allocated | Cumulative | Notes |
|------|-------|------------------|-----------|-------|
| **1** | Inquiry, Context, Problem Definition | 2.5 | 2.5 | Design brief, stakeholder intro |
| **2** | Research & Data Collection | 2.5 | 5 | User interviews, market research, constraints |
| **3** | Analysis & Synthesis | 2.5 | 7.5 | Empathy maps, affinity diagrams, insights |
| **4** | Ideation & Exploration | 2.5 | 10 | Ideation tools, concept sketching, narrowing |
| **5** | Concept Development | 2.5 | 12.5 | Detailed design, technical planning |
| **6** | Prototyping (First Iteration) | 2.5 | 15 | Making, assembly, basic testing |
| **7** | Testing & Iteration | 2.5 | 17.5 | User feedback, refinement, second iteration |
| **8** | Finalization, Documentation, Exhibition | 2.5 | 20 | Portfolio prep, presentation, showcase |

**Student progression check-ins:**
- End of Week 2: 5+ data points gathered (research quality check)
- End of Week 4: 3–5 viable concepts defined (ideation progress check)
- End of Week 6: Functional prototype exists (making progress check)
- End of Week 7: Testing feedback documented (iteration evidence)

## TABLE 4: Cognitive Load Caps by Age/Complexity

### Recommended Work-Rest Cycles (Pomodoro-Informed)

| Subject / Activity Type | Work Duration | Break Duration | Rationale |
|------------------------|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Pure desk work** (reading, writing, analysis) | 25–30 min | 5 min | Brain needs reset |
| **High-cognitive-demand** (problem-solving, design) | 30–40 min | 5–10 min | Design requires sustained focus; longer break needed |
| **Hands-on making** (prototyping, building) | 45–60 min | 10–15 min | Momentum important; longer immersion possible |
| **Collaboration/group work** | 20–30 min | 5 min | Group dynamics fatigue faster |
| **Testing/feedback** | 15–20 min | 5 min | High-cognitive, often frustrating |

### Adolescent Attention Span Guidelines

| Age | Direct Instruction Max | Notes |
|-----|----------------------|-------|
| **11–12 (Y7)** | 10–12 min | Puberty onset; still building working memory |
| **13–15 (Y8–9)** | 12–15 min | Working memory nearly adult; variable engagement |
| **16–18 (Y10+)** | 15–20 min | Adult-level capacity; still benefit from breaks |

**Empirical rule:** New learning should not exceed (1 + student's age in years). For 12-year-olds: max 13 minutes of continuous new instruction.

## TABLE 5: Handling Early Finishers & Differentiation

### Extensions for Students Who Finish Investigation Phase Early (Week 2–3 of 6-week unit)

| Extension Type | Time Required | Pedagogical Value | Example |
|----------------|----------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Interview 2 more stakeholders** | 30–45 min | Broader perspective; richer context | Talk to end-user + retailer + manufacturer |
| **Analyze 3 competitor products** | 30 min | Market research; differentiation insights | Compare features, cost, aesthetics of 3 existing solutions |
| **Create secondary empathy map** | 20 min | Multi-user perspective | Map the retailer's experience, not just customer |
| **Trend research in chosen domain** | 20–30 min | Future-focused thinking | What's emerging in sustainable packaging? Smart furniture? |
| **Accessibility audit of current products** | 25 min | Inclusive design | Evaluate 2 products for accessibility; suggest improvements |

**Teacher mindset:** Extensions are NOT "extra work" but **deepening the investigation**. They support the main deliverable.

---

### Extensions for Students Who Finish Ideation Early (Week 4 of 6-week unit)

| Extension Type | Time Required | Purpose | Example |
|----------------|----------------|---------|---------|
| **SCAMPER on one concept** | 20–25 min | Deepen one promising idea | Take your top concept; how would you Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other use, Eliminate, Reverse? |
| **Rapid prototyping (3 versions)** | 30 min | Explore multiple directions before committing | Build 3 quick paper/foam prototypes of the top 3 concepts |
| **Peer feedback session** | 15 min | Get external critique early | Present 3 concepts to peer group; capture feedback |
| **Constraint-based ideation** | 20 min | Push creative boundaries | How would you design this with only $5 budget? Or if it must weigh < 100g? |
| **Technical feasibility study** | 25 min | Reality-check the concept | Research materials, manufacturing methods, assembly process |

## TABLE 6: Debrief/Critique Structures with Timing

### 10-Minute Debrief (Whole-Class, End of Work Time)
| Component | Time | Details |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Share prompt** | 1 min | "Who wants to share what they discovered today?" |
| **Student shares** (2–3 volunteers) | 5–6 min | 1–2 min per student |
| **Teacher synthesis** | 2–3 min | "Here's what I noticed across the class..." |
| **Bridge to next lesson** | 1 min | "Next time, we'll take these ideas and..." |

**Best practice:** Use a timer; announce it: "You have 2 minutes to share."

---

### 15-Minute Structured Feedback (Pairs or Small Group)
| Phase | Time | Protocol |
|-------|------|----------|
| **Presenter frames work** | 3 min | "Here's what I was trying to do. My main challenge is..." |
| **Listeners ask clarifying questions** | 3 min | "Can you tell us more about...?" (No suggestions yet) |
| **Feedback (structured)** | 6 min | "I like..." (2 min) + "I wish..." (2 min) + "I wonder..." (2 min) |
| **Presenter response** | 2–3 min | "Thanks. I'm taking away..." |

---

### 45–60 Minute Design Critique (Dedicated Session)
| Phase | Time | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Presenter framing** | 3–5 min | Who is user? What goal? Constraints? What feedback seeking? |
| **Silent observation** (if visual) | 3–5 min | Viewers examine work; jot thoughts |
| **Clarifying questions** | 5–10 min | Critiquers ask for understanding (not judgment) |
| **Feedback** | 15–20 min | Structured: stickers/markers for "what works," "needs rethinking" |
| **Analysis & synthesis** | 10 min | Move through flagged areas; deeper discussion |
| **Note capture** | 3–5 min | Note-taker records key insights |
| **Wrap-up** | 2 min | Presenter summarizes takeaways |
| **Total** | **45–60 min** | Works for 1–2 designs; use for major iterations |

## TABLE 7: Common Lesson Pacing Mistakes & Corrections

| Mistake | Impact | Fix |
|---------|--------|-----|
| **Mini-lesson > 20 min** | Student disengagement by min 15; work time rushed | Reduce to 10–15 min; teach embedded "just-in-time" during work time |
| **Work time < 25 min** | Students can't get into flow; no time for meaningful making | Protect at least 30 min uninterrupted; combine 2 periods if needed |
| **No debrief** | Learning not consolidated; students unclear on progress | Add 5-min reflection minimum; even an exit ticket counts |
| **Debrief = open sharing** | Runs over; unfocused; some students don't share | Use **structured feedback protocol** with timer |
| **One-size-timing** | Fast students bored; slow students rushed | Build **extension menu** and **flexible checkpoints** into lesson plan |
| **Transitions unmapped** | 5–10 min of chaos between activities | Pre-plan: "At X o'clock, we stop and move to Y." Set a timer. |
| **Testing phase too short** | Insufficient data; students prototype again in Week 6; behind schedule | Allocate 2 full periods for testing in Week 5; allow 1-week iteration |
| **Exhibition last-minute** | Rushed; students don't reflect; learning goes un-captured | Start portfolio/documentation in Week 4; exhibition practice in Week 7 |

## TABLE 10: Teacher Facilitation Load During Work Time

### What Should Teacher Be Doing During 40-Minute Work Time (60-min period)

| Activity | Duration | Frequency | Notes |
|----------|----------|-----------|-------|
| **Circulate & observe** | 25–30 min | Continuous | Eyes on every student; spot struggles early |
| **1-on-1 conferences** | 10–15 min | 4–5 per period | 2–3 min per student; check in on progress |
| **Troubleshoot** | 3–5 min | As needed | Student stuck on tool, material, idea |
| **Whole-group mini-teach** (if needed) | 3–5 min | 0–1 per period | "I notice 3 of you have the same question..." |
| **Setup/cleanup/admin** | 2–3 min | Start/end | Distribute materials; collect work |

**Total teacher focus:** ~40 minutes of active facilitation = 95% of work time engaged with students.

**Passive teacher time:** Should be near zero. If teacher is sitting at desk, students are likely off-task.

## SUMMARY: Key Timing Rules of Thumb

1. **Mini-lesson ≤ 15 minutes** (max 20 for Y10+)
2. **Work time ≥ 30 minutes uninterrupted** (longer is better for design)
3. **Debrief minimum 5 minutes** (structured, not open)
4. **Transitions 2–3 minutes each** (pre-announced, timed)
5. **Per-student conference 2–3 minutes** (during work time)
6. **Design cycles should run 6–8 weeks** (2–3 weeks per major phase)
7. **Cognitive load breaks every 30–40 min** (for desk work) or every 45–60 min (for hands-on making)
8. **Early finisher extension ≥ 20 minutes** (not "busy work")
9. **Peer feedback session 10–15 minutes** (structured protocol)
10. **Major critique 45–60 minutes** (designed for depth)
