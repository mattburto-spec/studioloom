# StudioLoom Competitive Snapshot — What Matters

**TL;DR:** You have real competitive advantages in design pedagogy and per-step AI personalization. But zero customers means the advantages are theoretical. Customer validation is the blocker, not product quality.

---

## The Honest Assessment

### What You Do Better Than Everyone

**1. Per-step AI personalization** (only StudioLoom)
- SCAMPER gives divergent feedback ("what else? wilder ideas")
- PMI gives convergent feedback ("analyze the tradeoff")
- Five Whys detects shallow answers and pushes for depth
- No competitor does this. Khanmigo asks "what do you think?" the same way for everything.
- **Competitive window: 6-12 months before Toddle/MagicSchool figure it out**
- **Market impact: High IF you can show teachers the difference in student work**

**2. Workshop Model timing engine** (only StudioLoom)
- 4-phase structure validated against research
- 1+age instruction cap (Year 7 = max 13 min direct instruction)
- Auto-repair when the AI gets it wrong
- Teacher can drag/resize phases post-generation
- No competitor does this. Khanmigo, MagicSchool, Curipod don't validate timing.
- **Competitive window: 12-18 months (requires architecture rethink)**
- **Market impact: High IF schools care about research-backed timing**

**3. Design-specific AI that knows IB MYP design cycle**
- Design Teaching Corpus wired into every prompt
- Knowledge base stores teacher uploads and uses them for generation
- Per-class NM (Melbourne Metrics) assessment integration
- No competitor does this for design specifically.
- **Competitive window: 6-12 months**
- **Market impact: Medium (IB/GCSE market is niche)**

### What You Do the Same / Worse Than Competitors

**Generic stuff where you lose:**
- **Speed:** MagicSchool generates lessons in 2 minutes, you take intention + design
- **Breadth:** MagicSchool has 80 tools for any subject, you have 12 tools for design
- **Adoption:** Canva is free, ManageBac is the incumbent in IB schools, Khanmigo is Khan Academy
- **Distribution:** You have 0 customers, Toddle has 1000+ IB schools

## Real Threats (Ranked by Danger)

### 🔴 CRITICAL: Toddle

**Why:** Already in IB schools. Just added "AI-powered lesson generation." Has school-level buy-in.

**What makes it dangerous:**
- Teachers already use Toddle for curriculum planning + eCoursework + portfolios
- If Toddle adds student-facing AI mentoring, it's a Trojan horse (one platform does everything)
- It will be free/included because Toddle is already a subscription service

**What you should do:**
- Monitor Toddle's product roadmap religiously
- If Toddle announces "AI design cycle tutor," pivot immediately
- Your only defense is "we're better at design pedagogy" — but that's hard to prove

---

### 🟠 HIGH: MagicSchool

**Why:** 6M educators globally, 80 tools in one interface, fast generation.

**Weakness:** No design-specific knowledge. Lessons are generic templates.

**Threat:** They could add design-specific prompts + per-step rules in 2-3 months once they realize it's a gap.

**Your advantage:** You're ahead on the research. They'll catch up on engineering.

---

### 🟡 MEDIUM: Curipod

**Why:** Interactive + social + real-time feedback + teacher analytics. Teachers love it.

**Weakness:** Teacher-led only (no self-directed student agency). No design cycle structure.

**Threat:** Low unless they add self-directed mode. Your Open Studio is whitespace they haven't entered.

---

### 🟢 LOW: Khanmigo, Diffit, Canva

**Why:** Different problems. Generic, not design-specific.

**Threat:** Only if your market is "any teacher" not "design teachers." It's not.

- **Open Studio** = Real but niche. Only works if you first solve the "how do teachers enable self-directed learning?" problem.

## The Real Problem

You've built a strong product. But you have **zero market validation**. Everything in this analysis assumes:
- Teachers want design-specific AI (maybe they don't)
- Teachers care about per-step feedback tone (maybe they don't notice)
- Teachers will adopt yet another platform (they already have ManageBac/Google Classroom)
- Schools will replace Toddle (the incumbent)

**The only person who knows the answer is a real teacher who's tried it.**

---

## What to Do (Next 2 Weeks)

### 1. Set Up 10 Customer Conversations

Talk to MYP Design teachers (not design consultants, actual teachers with classes):
- How do you currently teach the design cycle?
- What tools do you use? (ManageBac? Google Classroom? Spreadsheets?)
- What frustrates you?
- What would make you switch?
- Does the AI feedback on your designs matter to you?

**Expected outcome:** Validation or invalidation of market assumptions.

### 2. Prepare a 5-Minute Demo

Focus on **one thing teachers would notice immediately:**
- Show SCAMPER feedback ("generate more ideas!") vs. PMI feedback ("analyze the tradeoff")
- OR show how the timing engine caught a lesson that was over the 1+age cap and auto-repaired it
- NOT: "We have 42 tools!" (Canva has that too)

### 3. Define Your Real Positioning

**Current:** "AI learning platform for MYP Design students with 42 tools"
**Better:** "Design-specific AI mentor that adapts its feedback based on the design phase (ideation vs. evaluation vs. analysis)"

---

## The Uncomfortable Truth

You've invented something real (per-step AI personalization). But you haven't proven anyone wants it at scale. All the competitive analysis in the world doesn't matter until you've had 10 real conversations with teachers who say "yes, I'd pay for that."

The next 3 months are about talking, not building. Every hour spent on new features is an hour not spent on discovering if the market exists.
